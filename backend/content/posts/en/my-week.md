Let's just crash and burn.

I've been overthinking things lately.

It's made me smaller. More afraid.

Honestly, the environment kind of does that to you.

Let me walk through my week.

Monday, I go to work.

I wake up at 5 in the morning.

I catch the bus in front of my place at 5:35.

I get to the office around 6:30.

I take my pill. I use the bathroom.

I work until 12:20.

When there's testing, sometimes it bleeds past 12:30.

Lunch ends at 1.

Then I work until 4:36.

If there's overtime, it's 6:06.

Then I take the bus home.

Hmm.

I get home around 7:50.

I usually end up on my phone.

Before I know it, it's 10.

Not because I want to. To turn my brain off.

Then I shower with cold water.

I fall asleep with this nagging feeling that I missed something.

*Ugh*, it's 5 in the morning.

Saturday comes and I sleep in.

Then I do something — not because I want to, but to stop thinking.

Though I'm usually at the office on Saturdays too.

At this rate I'll be rich.

Sunday is church.

I drive for the morning service shuttle.

I handle the sound booth in the morning.

I drive again for the afternoon return shuttle.

And just like that, the day is gone.

*Ugh*, it's 5 in the morning.

Doing this on repeat, I've slowly been losing something.

That's why I've gotten smaller. More afraid.

But what am I supposed to do about it.

Stop being scared. Just live.

I came here with nothing anyway — what exactly is there to lose?

Do the things I want to do.
