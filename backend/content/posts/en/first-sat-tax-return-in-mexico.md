---
title: My First SAT Tax Return in Mexico
date: 2026-05-01
---

Mexico's tax authority is the SAT (Servicio de Administración Tributaria). The annual tax return deadline is April 30th, and this year I filed one for the first time.

The SAT portal requires something called e.firma — essentially a digital certificate, similar to the ones used in Korea. It's a file issued when you first register as a worker here. I had no idea where I'd put it. After digging around for a while, I found it on a USB drive.

I uploaded the file, entered the password, and got an error.

> *Certificado, clave privada o contraseña de clave privada inválidos.*

Certificate, private key, or private key password is invalid.

I tried the password a few more times. Same error. It took me a while to realize that the e.firma password is separate from the SAT portal login password.

Eventually I tracked down the right password, tried again, and it went through. The portal pulled in my income automatically. No additional input needed — just hit submit.

There was no reason to panic. Like most things, it turned out fine.
