## Feel Before Tools

Most people starting out with motion graphics wonder: "Should I learn After Effects first?" AE is a powerful tool, of course — but developing a **sense of movement** matters more than mastering any software.

Good motion is built from timing, weight, and rhythm.

## Understanding Easing Gets You Halfway There

The biggest gap between beginners and intermediate motion designers is easing. Objects moving with linear easing look mechanical; objects with proper ease-in/ease-out feel alive.

- **ease-in**: Starts slow, ends fast — feels like something running away
- **ease-out**: Starts fast, ends slow — feels like landing
- **ease-in-out**: Smooth start and end — the most natural default

## Start Small

Don't try to build something complex from the start. Begin with moving a single simple shape. A bouncing circle. Text that fades in. These small exercises accumulate into the foundation for building complex sequences.

> Great motion makes the viewer forget they're watching motion at all.
